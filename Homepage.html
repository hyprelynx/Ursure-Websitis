Jugger at Berkeley is California's oldest jugger club. Originally founded in 2014 by graduate students under Berkeley Riot, the club has been running by various different monikers and run by many different groups of people until settling on its current name the club name, with our flagship team branded 'Ursae Majoris'.

Jugger is a five-a-side team sport involving facets of capture the flag and sparring, with both teams fighting to get a 'skull' into the enemy teams goal. It is played worldwide, originating in Germany, but now having spread to Australia, Asia, and obviously America, with the country hosting around 35 recognized teams across the states.

We host practices every weekend during college time, and host 2-3 midweeks over the course of the week, all on Memorial Glade on Berkeley Campus. Our leadership excels and greatly enjoys teaching new players the foundations of the game as much as the nuances of more complex gameplay. All members of the public are welcome to attend any of our practices.
